 Node is your solution.

We understand the need for web applications that handle large amounts of data efficiently. It is a requirement of any organization in 2014. Like Yahoo!, Paypal, eBay, and Uber, we love Node.js. Node is a powerful technology used to create efficient and scalable network applications. And because we love Node, we stay on top of the latest movements. From Mocha to Express to Hapi, we love it all. Using Hapi makes our projects quick to build, easy to deploy, and able to handle enormous amounts of data. The result is powerful applications with clean and maintainable code.

 Well-oiled applications.

As browser capabilities improve, users have less and less patience for anything... "behind the times". If you want to hold their attention for longer than a few seconds, you have to keep your interfaces looking sharp and up-to-date with modern technology and a relevant design. It's a lot to keep up with, but that's where Heather and our front-end team can help. We use our expertise in HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript to create beautiful, intuitive, interactive experiences that will keep your users coming back for more. Meanwhile, the backend team makes sure your new stuff integrates seamlessly with proprietary APIs, legacy databases, and any other techno-bogeymen reckless enough to challenge us.

 User-centric design.

In 2014, responsive design is more than a trend; it's a requirement. Nowadays, targeting iPhone, iPad, and Android means supporting at least 12 different resolutions. Not to mention that most of these devices can be flipped into landscape and portrait mode. But responsive implementation and design are relatively new skill sets, and many product/marketing teams don't have relevant expertise in-house... yet. Balderdash specializes in accelerating product teams past this hump. We focus on two critical areas: (1) reducing implementation risks and (2) setting up front-end code you can maintain without us.

 API-first development.

If you want an app that's future-proof, its functionality can't be tied to a single device. But that's NOT WHY you should build a service-oriented architecture! By powering your app with an API layer, you write reusable, testable, maintainable code -- instead of hacks. You don't have to completely reinvent your software to adapt to new technology -- your product was built on a solid foundation from the beginning. Most importantly, even if it's just a wrapper around an existing system, exposing and documenting a clean API around your business logic means less lock-in and fewer points of failure.
