Our Model

The course's self-funding model will have two main streams of income: renting workspace to our graduates (and other developers), and sponsorship from companies who wish to employ them. Some of our previous graduates are currently working on a complex Node-based content management system after 16-weeks.

The story so far

  • Jan '13

    Our story began in the British Library, with our Founder, Dan, trying to brush up on the latest technologies. However, self study can be a slow and arduous journey. With this in mind, Dan began arranging meetups, to gather together other developers, in order to create a peer-based learning environment.


    As the success of these meetups grew, so did his vision for what could be accomplished within this collaborative model. By working together, these groups were powering through the material faster than ever, with a better comprehension of the material than he could have ever imagined.

  • Oct '13

    When he met with Simon, the Chief Executive of Camden Town Unlimited, the potential for this model was quickly identified. Simon Pitkeathley asks the question, “If we gave you a room for your meetups what would you do there?” Simon arranged a work space for Dan, and together they began working towards creating an intensive coding bootcamp for complete beginners.

  • Jan '14

    Dan begins a pilot six-week bootcamp “Coding the Web” in Collective at 159 Camden High Street.

  • March '14

    Camden Town Unlimited funds a follow-up bootcamp at 159.

  • June '14

    The Collective Academy moves to 26 Camden HighStreet and Dan switches to project-based, peer-led learning.

  • August '14

    Wahome Muchiri at Africa Media Labs agrees to contract three of our recent graduates to build a complex Node-based content management system, mentored by Campbell Morgan, demonstrating that eight weeks might be (just) long enough preperation for life as a self-employed full-stack web developer if first- class technical mentors are available.

  • September '14

    Our fourth course begins.

  • November '14

    Our fourth course ends, but none of our students want to leave. We resolve to find premises and open a school and co-working space.


    17th - our Crowdfunding campaign launches.
